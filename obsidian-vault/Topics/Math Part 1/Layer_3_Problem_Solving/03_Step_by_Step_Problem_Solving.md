### Problem Type 1: Equivalence Relation ko check karna 🦸‍♂️

**Sawaal:** Prove karo ki integers (Z) ke set mein ek relation $R = \{(a, b) : 2 \text{ divides } (a - b)\}$ ek **Equivalence Relation** hai.

**Socho aise:** Ek superhero club banane ke liye 3 rules paas karne hote hain. Agar koi relation ye teen test pass kar le, toh wo **Equivalence Relation** ban jata hai! 

**Step 1: Reflexive Test (Khud ka dost)**
*   **Rule:** Har element ka khud ke saath relation hona chahiye, yani $(a, a) \in R$.
*   **Solve:** Agar hum 'a' mein se 'a' minus karein, toh $a - a = 0$ aayega. Aur zero toh 2 se divide hota hi hai! Isliye ye **Reflexive** hai.
*   *Analogy:* Jaise Batman khud ka sabse bada fan aur dost hai! 🦇

**Step 2: Symmetric Test (Tu mera dost, main tera dost)**
*   **Rule:** Agar $(a, b) \in R$ hai, toh $(b, a) \in R$ bhi hona chahiye.
*   **Solve:** Agar $(a - b)$ number 2 se divide hota hai (jaise $4 - 2 = 2$), toh $(b - a)$ (jaise $2 - 4 = -2$) bhi 2 se divide hoga. Isliye ye **Symmetric** hai.
*   *Analogy:* Agar Superman Flash ki help karta hai, toh Flash bhi Superman ki help karega! ⚡

**Step 3: Transitive Test (Dost ka dost, mera dost)**
*   **Rule:** Agar $(a, b) \in R$ aur $(b, c) \in R$ hai, toh $(a, c) \in R$ hona chahiye.
*   **Solve:** Maan lo $a - b$ aur $b - c$ dono 2 se divide hote hain. Agar hum dono ko jod dein: $(a - b) + (b - c) = a - c$. Ye naya number bhi 2 se divide hoga! Toh ye **Transitive** bhi ho gaya.
*   *Analogy:* Iron Man ka dost Thor hai, aur Thor ka dost Hulk hai. Toh Iron Man aur Hulk bhi dost ban gaye! 🤖🔨

**Conclusion:** Kyunki isne teeno test (**Reflexive**, **Symmetric**, **Transitive**) paas kar liye, ye relation ek perfect **Equivalence Relation** hai! 🎉

### Problem Type 2: Injective (One-one) aur Surjective (Onto) check karna 🚀

**Sawaal:** Show karo ki ek function $f : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$, jo $f(x) = 2x$ se diya gaya hai, wo **Injective** aur **Surjective** dono hai.

**Socho aise:** Hum space mission par hain! **Domain** (x) hamare Astronauts hain aur **Range** (y) unke Spaceships hain.

**Step 1: Injective Check karna**
*   **Rule:** Har $x$ (astronaut) ke paas apna khud ka ek alag spaceship hona chahiye. Koi sharing nahi!
*   **Solve:** Maan lo do logon ka answer same aa gaya: $f(x_1) = f(x_2)$.
    Iska matlab: $2x_1 = 2x_2$.
    Dono side se 2 hata do, toh bacha: $x_1 = x_2$.
    Wow! Iska matlab agar spaceship same hai, toh astronaut bhi wahi ek hi insaan hai. Har insaan ka apna unique spaceship hai. Toh ye function **Injective** hai.

**Step 2: Surjective Check karna**
*   **Rule:** **Range** (Spaceship parking) mein koi bhi spaceship khali nahi rehni chahiye. Har $y$ ke liye pichhe ek $x$ hona zaroori hai.
*   **Solve:** Maan lo ek spaceship $y$ hai. Humein dekhna hai ki usme kaun baithega.
    Formula hai: $y = 2x$. 
    Toh $x$ kya hoga? $x = \frac{y}{2}$.
    Kyunki $y$ ek real number hai, toh $\frac{y}{2}$ bhi hamesha ek real number hi hoga. Yani har $y$ (spaceship) ke liye ek $x$ (astronaut) maujood hai! Toh ye function **Surjective** hai.

**Conclusion:** Kyunki ye function **Injective** aur **Surjective** dono hai, hum isko Bijective function bhi kehte hain! 🌌
